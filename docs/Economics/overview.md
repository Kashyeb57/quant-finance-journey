# Economics

Micro and macroeconomics, monetary vs. fiscal policy, and the economic context behind markets.

## What economics means for a quant

Markets don't move in a vacuum — they price expectations about growth, inflation, and policy. A quant doesn't need to be a macroeconomist, but the models don't hold without the context: interest rates set the discount factor in every valuation, central-bank policy moves the whole yield curve, and regime shifts break correlations that a backtest assumed were stable. Economics is the layer that explains *why* the inputs to your models change.
