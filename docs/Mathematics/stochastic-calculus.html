Stochastic Calculus

Brownian motion, Ito's Lemma, SDEs, and martingales for modeling market randomness.

The single most important SDE in finance is geometric Brownian motion — the model behind Black–Scholes:

dSt=μSt dt+σSt dWtdS_t = \mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_tdSt​=μSt​dt+σSt​dWt​

Its solution (via Itô's Lemma) is

St=S0exp⁡ ⁣[(μ−σ22)t+σWt]S_t = S_0 \exp\!\left[\left(\mu - \tfrac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t\right]St​=S0​exp[(μ−2σ2​)t+σWt​]

Reading that formula is one thing — watching it is another:

Monte CarloGeometric Brownian Motion simulator

Every blue line is one possible future: dSₜ = μSₜdt + σSₜdWₜ, starting at S₀ = $100. The dashed red curve is the expected path — note how little any single path resembles it. Crank σ up and watch the fan explode; hit Re-simulate for a fresh set of futures.

Things to notice while you play:

  1. The expected path is a lie you never live. The dashed red curve is E[Sₜ] = S₀e^μᵗ, yet almost no individual path tracks it. Averages hide the violence of randomness.
  2. The −σ²/2 correction is real. Set μ = 0% and σ = 60%: more than half the paths finish below S₀ even though the drift is zero. That's the volatility drag term (μ − σ²/2) at work.
  3. Volatility scales with √t. The fan of paths widens like a square root, not a straight line — double the horizon and uncertainty only grows by √2.
  4. Paths are jagged everywhere. Brownian motion is continuous but nowhere differentiable — there's no slope to measure, which is precisely why ordinary calculus fails and Itô calculus exists.

The full study roadmap — 17 topics​

My complete stochastic-calculus study plan, based on the excellent roadmap by Quantify Your Career (watch the video for the study sequence). Each topic lists what it covers and what it unlocks — expand as you go:

0 · Foundational concepts — measures P vs Q, numéraire, Greeks

ODE vs PDE vs SDE and how they map into each other (SDE → generator → PDE via Feynman–Kac). Price vs value function. P vs Q: the real-world measure for forecasting/risk, the risk-neutral measure for pricing. Numéraire choice. Greeks as local sensitivities, with pathwise vs likelihood-ratio estimators in Monte Carlo.

Needs: calculus, linear algebra, probability, Python. Unlocks: everything below.

1 · Brownian motion, filtrations & martingales

The canonical noise WtW_tWt​: quadratic variation [W]t=t[W]_t = t[W]t​=t (the foundation of Itô calculus), correlated Brownians with d⟨W1,W2⟩t=ρ dtd\langle W^1, W^2\rangle_t = \rho\,dtd⟨W1,W2⟩t​=ρdt. Adaptedness — no peeking ahead. Martingales as fair games, the engine of no-arbitrage.

Needs: probability, normal distribution, conditional expectation.

2 · Itô calculus — integral, lemma, local time

The Itô integral (isometry, zero mean), Itô's lemma in 1-D and multi-D, Tanaka's formula and local time for kinked payoffs like ∣x∣|x|∣x∣. Itô vs Stratonovich — finance uses Itô because it respects the information flow.

Needs: (1). Unlocks: SDE solutions, pricing PDEs, Greeks.

3 · SDE theory & canonical diffusions — GBM, OU, CIR, CEV

Existence/uniqueness (Lipschitz + linear growth), strong vs weak solutions. The model zoo: GBM (equities — the simulator above!), OU/Vasicek (mean reversion), CIR (positive rates/variance, Feller condition 2κθ≥ξ22\kappa\theta \ge \xi^22κθ≥ξ2), CEV, local vol.

Needs: (1)(2). Unlocks: risk-neutral pricing, PDEs, Monte Carlo.

4 · Measure change & risk-neutral pricing — Girsanov

Radon–Nikodym densities as exponential martingales, Girsanov's theorem (drift shifts, volatility doesn't), bank-account vs forward measures, change-of-numéraire consistency.

Needs: (1)(2)(3). Unlocks: Feynman–Kac pricing, rates modeling.

5 · Generators, Feynman–Kac & Kolmogorov — the PDE link

The generator Lf=ft+μfx+12σ2fxx\mathcal{L}f = f_t + \mu f_x + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f_{xx}Lf=ft​+μfx​+21​σ2fxx​ drives the backward pricing PDE and the forward Fokker–Planck density PDE. Boundary conditions (absorbing/reflecting), discounting terms, HJB preview.

Needs: (2)(3)(4). Unlocks: PDE solvers, American options, control.

6 · Greeks, replication & hedging

Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho; self-financing replication; discrete-hedging slippage proportional to Δt\sqrt{\Delta t}Δt​; pathwise vs likelihood-ratio Greeks; anatomy of hedging error (model, discretization, costs).

Needs: (2)(4)(5). Unlocks: calibration validation, risk P&L.

7 · Jumps & Lévy models — Merton, Bates, VG/NIG

Compound Poisson jumps, the Lévy triplet, Itô with jumps, and Fourier/COS pricing via characteristic functions — how models capture crash risk and fat tails.

Needs: (2)(3)(4).

8 · Stochastic vol, local vol & hybrids — Heston, SABR, SLV

Heston (CIR variance, correlation, Feller condition), SABR (the swaption-desk standard with its closed-form smile approximation), and SLV hybrids that calibrate a leverage function to the surface while keeping realistic dynamics.

Needs: (3)(4)(5)(14).

9 · Rough volatility — optional depth

Volterra SDEs with memory kernels, rough Bergomi reproducing steep short-maturity skews, simulation via circulant embedding/Cholesky, and why hedges get twitchier.

Needs: (8) + fractional-calculus awareness.

10 · Optimal stopping & American options

The Snell envelope (smallest supermartingale dominating the payoff), Longstaff–Schwartz least-squares Monte Carlo, obstacle-problem PDEs with PSOR/penalty methods.

Needs: (5)(6)(14).

11 · Stochastic control, HJB, BSDEs & FBSDEs

Controlled SDEs and the Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman equation, verification theorems, Pontryagin's principle, backward SDEs solved by regression or deep learning.

Needs: (5)(6). Unlocks: portfolio/consumption problems, execution, xVA.

12 · Term structure of rates — short-rate, HJM, LMM

Vasicek/CIR/Hull–White with exponential-affine bond prices (Riccati ODEs), the HJM no-arbitrage drift condition, and the Libor Market Model calibrated caplets-first with a SABR overlay for smiles.

Needs: (3)(4)(5).

13 · Filtering & latent states — Kalman, particle filters

Kalman filtering (linear-Gaussian), EKF/UKF for nonlinear models, Kushner–Stratonovich nonlinear filtering, particle filters with resampling — inferring hidden vol/rates/intensity from noisy prices.

Needs: (3) + time-series inference.

14 · Numerics & engines — SDE, PDE, MC, Fourier

Euler–Maruyama vs Milstein discretization, variance reduction (antithetic, control variates, MLMC), Crank–Nicolson and ADI PDE schemes, Fourier/COS pricing with cumulant truncation. See Numerical Methods for the fundamentals.

Needs: (2)(3)(5). Unlocks: everything computational.

15 · Calibration, identifiability & model risk

The P vs Q calibration split, positivity/Feller/no-arbitrage constraints, regularization and priors, out-of-sample validation and stress tests — how a model earns risk sign-off.

Needs: (6)(8)(12)(14).

16 · Risk, exposures & xVA

Exposure profiles (EE, EPE, PFE), netting and collateral, wrong-way risk, and the xVA stack — CVA, DVA, FVA, KVA, MVA — where pricing meets counterparty credit and capital.

Needs: (4)(6)(7)(12)(14).

📌 Suggested path: master 0→6 in order (they build directly on each other), then branch by interest — equities-vol people go 7→8→9, rates people go 12, and everyone eventually needs 14 and 15.
