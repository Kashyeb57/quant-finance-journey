Matrix Factorization (PCA / SVD)

Eigenvalue decomposition, PCA, and SVD for dimensionality reduction and risk factors.

Key topics​

  • Eigenvalue decomposition — Σ=QΛQ⊤\Sigma = Q \Lambda Q^\topΣ=QΛQ⊤ for symmetric matrices
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) — rank risk factors by explained variance
  • Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) — A=UΣV⊤A = U \Sigma V^\topA=UΣV⊤, works for any matrix

Why quants care​

  • Dimensionality reduction: a universe of 500 stocks doesn't have 500 independent risks — a handful of components usually explain most of the variance.
  • Risk factor identification: in rates, the first three principal components of yield curve moves are famously level, slope, and curvature — together explaining ~95%+ of variation.
  • PCA is also the workhorse for cleaning noisy covariance matrices before portfolio optimization.
