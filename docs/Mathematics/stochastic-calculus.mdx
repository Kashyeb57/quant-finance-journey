# Stochastic Calculus

Brownian motion, Ito's Lemma, SDEs, and martingales for modeling market randomness.

The single most important SDE in finance is **geometric Brownian motion** — the model behind
Black–Scholes:

$$
dS_t = \mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t
$$

Its solution (via Itô's Lemma) is

$$
S_t = S_0 \exp\!\left[\left(\mu - \tfrac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t\right]
$$

Things to notice while you play:

1. **The expected path is a lie you never live.** The dashed red curve is E[Sₜ] = S₀e^μᵗ, yet almost
   no individual path tracks it. Averages hide the violence of randomness.
2. **The −σ²/2 correction is real.** Set μ = 0% and σ = 60%: more than half the paths finish *below*
   S₀ even though the drift is zero. That's the volatility drag term (μ − σ²/2) at work.
3. **Volatility scales with √t.** The fan of paths widens like a square root, not a straight line —
   double the horizon and uncertainty only grows by √2.
4. **Paths are jagged everywhere.** Brownian motion is continuous but nowhere differentiable —
   there's no slope to measure, which is precisely why ordinary calculus fails and Itô calculus exists.

## The full study roadmap — 17 topics

My complete stochastic-calculus study plan, based on the excellent roadmap by
[Quantify Your Career](https://youtu.be/whdFQLP4kO4) (watch the video for the study
sequence). Each topic lists what it covers and what it unlocks — expand as you go:

<details>
<summary><b>0 · Foundational concepts</b> — measures P vs Q, numéraire, Greeks</summary>

ODE vs PDE vs SDE and how they map into each other (SDE → generator → PDE via
Feynman–Kac). Price vs value function. **P vs Q**: the real-world measure for
forecasting/risk, the risk-neutral measure for pricing. Numéraire choice. Greeks as local
sensitivities, with pathwise vs likelihood-ratio estimators in Monte Carlo.

*Needs:* calculus, linear algebra, probability, Python. *Unlocks:* everything below.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>1 · Brownian motion, filtrations & martingales</b></summary>

The canonical noise $W_t$: quadratic variation $[W]_t = t$ (the foundation of Itô
calculus), correlated Brownians with $d\langle W^1, W^2\rangle_t = \rho\,dt$.
Adaptedness — no peeking ahead. Martingales as fair games, the engine of no-arbitrage.

*Needs:* probability, normal distribution, conditional expectation.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>2 · Itô calculus</b> — integral, lemma, local time</summary>

The Itô integral (isometry, zero mean), Itô's lemma in 1-D and multi-D, Tanaka's formula
and local time for kinked payoffs like $|x|$. Itô vs Stratonovich — finance uses Itô
because it respects the information flow.

*Needs:* (1). *Unlocks:* SDE solutions, pricing PDEs, Greeks.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>3 · SDE theory & canonical diffusions</b> — GBM, OU, CIR, CEV</summary>

Existence/uniqueness (Lipschitz + linear growth), strong vs weak solutions. The model
zoo: **GBM** (equities — the simulator above!), **OU/Vasicek** (mean reversion), **CIR**
(positive rates/variance, Feller condition $2\kappa\theta \ge \xi^2$), **CEV**, local vol.

*Needs:* (1)(2). *Unlocks:* risk-neutral pricing, PDEs, Monte Carlo.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>4 · Measure change & risk-neutral pricing</b> — Girsanov</summary>

Radon–Nikodym densities as exponential martingales, **Girsanov's theorem** (drift shifts,
volatility doesn't), bank-account vs forward measures, change-of-numéraire consistency.

*Needs:* (1)(2)(3). *Unlocks:* Feynman–Kac pricing, rates modeling.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>5 · Generators, Feynman–Kac & Kolmogorov</b> — the PDE link</summary>

The generator $\mathcal{L}f = f_t + \mu f_x + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f_{xx}$ drives the
backward pricing PDE and the forward Fokker–Planck density PDE. Boundary conditions
(absorbing/reflecting), discounting terms, HJB preview.

*Needs:* (2)(3)(4). *Unlocks:* PDE solvers, American options, control.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>6 · Greeks, replication & hedging</b></summary>

Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho; self-financing replication; discrete-hedging slippage
proportional to $\sqrt{\Delta t}$; pathwise vs likelihood-ratio Greeks; anatomy of hedging
error (model, discretization, costs).

*Needs:* (2)(4)(5). *Unlocks:* calibration validation, risk P&L.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>7 · Jumps & Lévy models</b> — Merton, Bates, VG/NIG</summary>

Compound Poisson jumps, the Lévy triplet, Itô with jumps, and Fourier/COS pricing via
characteristic functions — how models capture crash risk and fat tails.

*Needs:* (2)(3)(4).

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>8 · Stochastic vol, local vol & hybrids</b> — Heston, SABR, SLV</summary>

**Heston** (CIR variance, correlation, Feller condition), **SABR** (the swaption-desk
standard with its closed-form smile approximation), and **SLV** hybrids that calibrate a
leverage function to the surface while keeping realistic dynamics.

*Needs:* (3)(4)(5)(14).

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>9 · Rough volatility</b> — optional depth</summary>

Volterra SDEs with memory kernels, rough Bergomi reproducing steep short-maturity skews,
simulation via circulant embedding/Cholesky, and why hedges get twitchier.

*Needs:* (8) + fractional-calculus awareness.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>10 · Optimal stopping & American options</b></summary>

The Snell envelope (smallest supermartingale dominating the payoff), Longstaff–Schwartz
least-squares Monte Carlo, obstacle-problem PDEs with PSOR/penalty methods.

*Needs:* (5)(6)(14).

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>11 · Stochastic control, HJB, BSDEs & FBSDEs</b></summary>

Controlled SDEs and the Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman equation, verification theorems,
Pontryagin's principle, backward SDEs solved by regression or deep learning.

*Needs:* (5)(6). *Unlocks:* portfolio/consumption problems, execution, xVA.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>12 · Term structure of rates</b> — short-rate, HJM, LMM</summary>

Vasicek/CIR/Hull–White with exponential-affine bond prices (Riccati ODEs), the HJM
no-arbitrage drift condition, and the Libor Market Model calibrated caplets-first with a
SABR overlay for smiles.

*Needs:* (3)(4)(5).

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>13 · Filtering & latent states</b> — Kalman, particle filters</summary>

Kalman filtering (linear-Gaussian), EKF/UKF for nonlinear models,
Kushner–Stratonovich nonlinear filtering, particle filters with resampling — inferring
hidden vol/rates/intensity from noisy prices.

*Needs:* (3) + time-series inference.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>14 · Numerics & engines</b> — SDE, PDE, MC, Fourier</summary>

Euler–Maruyama vs Milstein discretization, variance reduction (antithetic, control
variates, MLMC), Crank–Nicolson and ADI PDE schemes, Fourier/COS pricing with cumulant
truncation. See [Numerical Methods](./numerical-methods) for the fundamentals.

*Needs:* (2)(3)(5). *Unlocks:* everything computational.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>15 · Calibration, identifiability & model risk</b></summary>

The P vs Q calibration split, positivity/Feller/no-arbitrage constraints, regularization
and priors, out-of-sample validation and stress tests — how a model earns risk sign-off.

*Needs:* (6)(8)(12)(14).

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>16 · Risk, exposures & xVA</b></summary>

Exposure profiles (EE, EPE, PFE), netting and collateral, wrong-way risk, and the xVA
stack — CVA, DVA, FVA, KVA, MVA — where pricing meets counterparty credit and capital.

*Needs:* (4)(6)(7)(12)(14).

</details>
