Discrete Mathematics

Combinatorics, graph theory, and discrete optimization for algorithms and HFT.

Key topics​

  • Combinatorics — counting arrangements, the backbone of probability
  • Graph theory — networks of nodes and edges
  • Discrete optimization — best choice from a finite (often huge) set

Why quants care​

  • Combinatorics feeds probability: binomial option-pricing trees literally count up-down path combinations; interview classics ("how many ways…") are combinatorics.
  • Graph theory models market structure: currency-arbitrage detection is a negative-cycle search, settlement networks and contagion are graph problems.
  • Discrete optimization appears wherever positions come in whole units: order routing, trade scheduling, and the algorithms behind high-frequency trading where data structures and O(·) complexity decide profitability.
