CAPM & Asset Pricing

The Capital Asset Pricing Model and the theory of risk and return.

CAPM's one-line claim: you only get paid for the risk you can't diversify away.

E[Ri]=Rf+βi (E[Rm]−Rf)E[R_i] = R_f + \beta_i\,(E[R_m] - R_f)E[Ri​]=Rf​+βi​(E[Rm​]−Rf​)
  • RfR_fRf​ — the risk-free rate (what you earn for taking no risk)
  • βi\beta_iβi​ — how much the asset moves when the market moves
  • E[Rm]−RfE[R_m] - R_fE[Rm​]−Rf​ — the market risk premium

Key terms — drill them​

term
Beta (β)
tap to reveal
definition
Sensitivity of an asset’s return to the market’s return: β = Cov(Rᵢ, Rₘ) / Var(Rₘ). β = 1 moves with the market, β > 1 amplifies it, β < 1 dampens it, β < 0 hedges it.
1 / 7

Worked example​

A stock has β = 1.4. With a risk-free rate of 3% and an expected market return of 8%:

E[R]=3%+1.4×(8%−3%)=10%E[R] = 3\% + 1.4 \times (8\% - 3\%) = 10\%E[R]=3%+1.4×(8%−3%)=10%

If your research says the stock will actually return 13%, CAPM says it offers +3% alpha — either you've found a bargain, or your forecast (or CAPM itself) is wrong. Quant careers are built on telling those two apart.
