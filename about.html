About

Who I am​

I'm Joyeb Kashyeb — a computer science student at Jacksonville State University with a concentration in data science, an international student, and someone deliberately building toward a career in quantitative finance. This site is my public workbench for that climb.

I'm currently open to internships and roles in quantitative finance, quantitative research, and data science. If you're hiring or just want to connect, the links below reach me directly.

Why I built this​

I learn by building. Reading a formula tells me almost nothing; implementing it, breaking it, and watching it respond to a slider is when it becomes mine. The rule I hold myself to across every page here: if I can't simulate or test an idea, I don't trust that I've understood it. That's why the Python runs in your browser, the pricing models have draggable inputs, and every finished topic ends with a quiz.

What I'm studying right now​

  • Stochastic calculus — Brownian motion, Itô's lemma, and the machinery under every pricing model.
  • Derivatives pricing — payoffs, binomial trees, Black–Scholes and the Greeks.
  • Bayesian inference — priors, posteriors, and MCMC, headed toward probabilistic models of returns.

What I've built​

Beyond the notes themselves: a browser-based Python notebook with numpy, pandas and matplotlib, a set of interactive pricing and probability labs, a live market terminal, and finance-focused coding projects like a FIFO stock ledger. The full list lives on the Projects page.

Where to find me​

The full source of this site lives on GitHub. If you spot an error in any note, the "Edit this page" link at the bottom of every doc opens the GitHub editor — corrections are welcome.
